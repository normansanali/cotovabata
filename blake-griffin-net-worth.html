Blake Griffin | Net Worth, NBA Earnings & Fines & Suspensions

NBA Contracts

In 2009, Blake signed a four-year $23.29 million rookie contract with Los Angeles Clippers.

According to the terms of the contract, Blake received $10,340,710 guaranteed at a sing and an average annual salary of $5,824,671.

After the rookie contract expired, the Clippers extended Blake’s stay with the team and signed a five years Bird extension worth $94,538,626.

The contract guaranteed $94,538,626 at sign with average annual salary of $18,907,725.

In 2017, Blake signed a one-year $18,367,653 extension with the Clippers but was retained and signed another five-year $171,174,820 Bird extension.

The contract is still one of the highest in NBA history and came with all the bells and whistles.

According to the contract, the entire amount of $171,174,820 was guaranteed at the sign with a $34,234,964 average annual salary.

In 2018, Blake was traded to Detroit Pistons.

Talking about his current contract in 2021, Blake Griffin signed a one-year $2,641,691 contract with the Nets, including $2,641,691 guaranteed and an annual average salary of $2,641,691.

When it’s all said and done, Blake has earned $255,764,829 from NBA salaries and bonuses to date.

Out of the total amount, he made $113,712,751 from his nine seasons with the Clippers, $138,180,711 from five seasons with the Pistons and $3,871,367 from two seasons with the Nets.
