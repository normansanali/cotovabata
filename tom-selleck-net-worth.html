Tom Selleck | Movies & TV Shows, Investments, and Endorsements

Movies & TV Shows

After a string of minor roles, Selleck landed on a recurring role in The Rockford Files. Following that, he appeared as a cowboy in the film The Sacketts.

Similarly, in 1980 Selleck landed the role, which made him the household hit in the TV series Magnum P.I.

The show was shot in Hawaii and continued for eight seasons.

The role also garnered him an Emmy for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series.

Even while he was on the show, he appeared in several other side projects like Three Men and a Baby and Runaway.

Following his successful part in Magnum P.I, he gained further recognition with films such as An Innocent Man, The Love Letter, In & Out, and Quigley Down Under.

He also appeared as the dashing ex-boyfriend of Monica Geller in the iconic sitcom Friends.

Selleck currently stars in a TV series Blue Bloods running since 2010.

Investments

Perhaps the biggest investment of the actor is his ranch in California.

The ranch spreads over 65 acres. He has more than 3000 avocado trees on the land.

Though he does not like fruit himself, he likes to grow it and sell it in the market.

He was involved in a controversy because of the avocado farm. Callegauas Municipal Water District sued him for improperly transferring around 1.4 million gallons of water from Calleguas Municipal Water District to his farm.

Selleck ultimately settled the lawsuit paying $21.685.55 to the Municipal Water District.

Moreover, Selleck was once the minority stakeholder of the Detroit Tigers.

Tom Selleck | Career

Tom Selleck was raised with his three siblings in Sherman Oaks. He graduated his high school in 1962.

Tom lived with his parents for a brief time after high school and attended Los Angeles Valley College. However, he won a basketball scholarship and enrolled at the University of Southern California.

Selleck was studying Business Administration, but while studying, his teachers encouraged him to try his hands in acting.

With the change of heart, Selleck dropped out of college to pursue an acting career.

It was during this time he was drafted into the US Army as part of the Vietnam conflict.

Selleck served in the California National Guard from 1967 to 1973 in the 160th Infantry Regiment.

After his stint in California National Guard, he returned to television and started appearing in TV commercials.
